# Project for NSBB552

## Background

Hypoxia is classified as insufficient oxygen or low O2 tension. Its pathophysiological consequences can include dysregulation of angiogenesis, glucose metabolism, and cell proliferation or survival. Gestational hypoxia and placental oxidative stress are associated with adverse neurodevelopmental outcomes.

A previous study examined whether treating the placenta could mitigate the effects of placental oxidative stress. That study used microRNA sequencing and RNA-seq on the whole fetal brain to test whether gestational hypoxia alters placental secretions, especially microRNAs, in ways that affect fetal brain development, and whether a mitochondria-targeted antioxidant (MitoQ nanoparticles) given to the mother can prevent those effects.

## Hypothesis

Treatment of the placenta with MitoQ to reduce hypoxic injury in the neonatal brain will result in differential expression of immunohistochemical markers in the neonatal brain, specifically markers associated with Rbfox3, OCLN, Iba1, GFAP, and GAP-43, when comparing normoxic and hypoxic conditions.

## Data set (or repository you will be using)

For support of this hypothesis, the project will use GSE86346 (placenta- and fetus-derived microRNAs) and GSE86408 (fetal whole-brain RNA-seq) for a secondary analysis of a rat model exposed to gestational hypoxia. These datasets were used to show that hypoxia alters placental secretions and fetal brain gene expression, and that maternal placental-targeted antioxidant treatment with MitoQ-NPs partially normalizes these changes.

These analyses will be used to define brain gene sets and corresponding immunohistochemical markers that will serve as the primary readouts in the proposed experimental model.

- [GSE86346](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE86346) — *Treating the placenta to prevent adverse effects of gestational hypoxia on fetal brain development* [microRNA]
- [GSE86408](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE86408) — *Treating the placenta to prevent adverse effects of gestational hypoxia on fetal brain development* [RNA-seq]

2. Use public omics datasets (RNA-seq and related data) to identify candidate pathways or markers, then justify focusing on those in in-house experiments.
